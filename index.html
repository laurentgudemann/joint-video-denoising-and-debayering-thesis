Abstract

This thesis aims to improve the quality of images captured by a digital motion picture camera by addressing two steps in the image processing pipeline: denoising and debayering. These tasks are essential for the reconstruction of detailed color images from the noisy Bayer data produced by the digital sensors.

Recently, deep learning-based methods have been demonstrated to outperform traditional algorithms in these disciples. Therefore, an overview is provided of convolutional and transformer-based neural network architectures, which are designed for computationally efficient computer vision and image restoration. Findings from literature are then incorporated into several deep neural network architectures, which perform joint denoising and debayering on raw data from an ARRI cinema camera. It is shown that the joint models can process the noisy Bayer images into denoised RGB images more efficiently than a series of models that are trained on denoising and debayering tasks separately. However, it is also observed that the joint models produce temporally inconsistent outputs if they are only given information from a single frame. To avoid such distracting flickering, an efficient temporal fusion mechanism was incorporated into modern single-image denoising architectures. The resulting spatiotemporal models achieve even greater computational efficiency than the spatial models while demonstrating significantly improved temporal consistency.

In addition, a novel method for generating clean ground truth from any raw video recording is proposed, which overcomes the limitations of existing datasets and dataset processing methods for supervised raw video denoising and debayering. This method is applied to newly recorded and pre-existing video data to obtain a raw video dataset suitable for the task. A set of computationally efficient models is then trained using the joint spatiotemporal architectures and the dataset. Their effectiveness is demonstrated on real footage and in comparison with industry-standard methods.
